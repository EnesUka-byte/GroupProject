Theme: Cry of Fear

Cry of Fear is a 2012 indie survival horror video game developed and published by Team Psykskallar. It originated as a mod for Half-Life developed by the same team a year earlier. The game follows the story of Simon Henriksson, a 19-year-old Swedish man suffering from depression and anxiety as he explores the eerie streets of Stockholm.

Combining elements of survival horror and first-person shooter gameplay, Cry of Fear challenges players to navigate through a haunting urban landscape filled with monsters and unsettling psychological imagery. Its story delves into themes of mental illness and trauma, earning praise for its atmospheric tension, narrative depth, and creative use of the Half-Life engine. The game received multiple accolades, including the Mod DB awards for Best Single Player Game of the Year and the Community Award.

Gameplay

In Cry of Fear, players control Simon Henriksson, who awakens in a dark alley in Stockholm shortly after being struck by a car. From a first-person perspective, players must solve puzzles, explore eerie environments, and fight disturbing creatures to survive. The game alternates between the real world and nightmarish dreamscapes, reminiscent of the Silent Hill series.
