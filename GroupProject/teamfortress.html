Theme: Team Fortress 2

Team Fortress 2 (TF2) is a multiplayer first-person shooter game developed and published by Valve Corporation in 2007. It is the sequel to the 1996 Team Fortress mod for Quake and its 1999 remake, Team Fortress Classic. It was released in October 2007 as part of The Orange Box for Microsoft Windows and the Xbox 360, and was ported to the PlayStation 3 in December 2007. It was released as a standalone game for Windows in April 2008, and later updated to support macOS in June 2010 and Linux in February 2013. It became free-to-play in June 2011 and is distributed through Valve’s Steam platform.

Players join one of two teams — RED or BLU — and choose from nine unique character classes to compete in game modes such as capture the flag and king of the hill. Its development was led by John Cook and Robin Walker, the developers of the original mod. Announced in 1998 as Team Fortress 2: Brotherhood of Arms, the project initially aimed for realistic, militaristic visuals and gameplay. Over nine years of development, it transformed into a colorful, stylized experience. When Valve went silent for six years, TF2 frequently appeared on Wired News’s annual “vaporware” list.

Finally released in 2007 using Valve’s Source Engine, Team Fortress 2 preserved the series’ class-based gameplay while introducing a bold, cartoonish art direction inspired by the works of J. C. Leyendecker, Dean Cornwell, and Norman Rockwell. The result was a distinctive 1960s spy film aesthetic and a strong focus on personality and humor for each playable class. Team Fortress 2 has received critical acclaim for its art style, gameplay, humor, and use of character in a wholly multiplayer game. Since its release, it has been celebrated as one of the greatest video games ever made, maintaining a passionate online community with fan animations, comics, and Valve’s annual Saxxy Awards film contest. It also stands as a key forerunner to the modern hero shooter genre, pioneering many of its core design principles.
